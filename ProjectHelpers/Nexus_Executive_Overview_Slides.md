## SLIDE 1 — The Vision: Why Nexus

**Title:** Nexus — An AI Teammate for Our Platform Team

**Subtitle / one-line vision:**
> Imagine our platform team's knowledge, expertise, and day-to-day Azure work — amplified by AI that can safely act, not just advise.

**The problem we live with today (left column):**
- Critical know-how lives in people's heads, not where the team can reach it
- Engineers repeat the same Azure tasks and re-solve solved problems
- Onboarding is slow — new people take months to get productive
- Work bottlenecks on a few key people, and outcomes vary by who picks it up

**The opportunity (right column):**
- Become an AI-native platform team — a step-change in how we operate
- Turn tribal knowledge into a shared, always-on capability
- Move faster with fewer escalations and less repetitive toil
- A strategic advantage in platform maturity, not just a tool

## SPEAKER NOTES

### Slide 1 (~2 minutes)
"I want to share something I've been thinking about — a concept I'm calling Nexus. Today, our platform team runs on a lot of manual effort and a lot of knowledge that lives in people's heads. When someone's out, that knowledge is out with them. We repeat the same Azure tasks over and over, we re-solve problems we've already solved, and onboarding a new engineer takes months. And too much of our work bottlenecks on a few key people — when they're busy or out, things stall, and outcomes can vary depending on who picks up the ticket.

Now imagine an AI teammate built specifically for us — one that knows our environment, our standards, and our way of working, and can actually help carry the load. That's the opportunity. This isn't just about saving a few clicks. It's about becoming an AI-native platform team — turning the knowledge trapped in individuals into a shared capability that's always on. That's a real strategic advantage in how mature and fast our platform operation can be. Let me show you what that would actually look like."

### Slide 2 (~2 minutes)
"The idea is simple. Three things come together. First, our own team knowledge — our docs, our standards, the way we do things. Second, a capable AI assistant on top of it. And third — and this is the important part — the ability to take safe, approved actions on Azure. Not just answer questions, but actually help do the work.

The thing I care most about is that center-right piece: every real action would be approval-gated. Nexus proposes, a human approves — it never just runs off and changes production on its own. So we get the speed of AI with the control we need.

What that delivers: it knows our environment, it acts safely, it can switch between expert personas depending on the task — from a simple helper to an architect — and it learns and improves over time. To be clear, this is a concept today, not a finished product. But the vision is clear, and it's very achievable. With your backing, my next step would be a focused pilot to prove the value — and that's really what I'm here to ask for."
